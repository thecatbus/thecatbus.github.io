Hello! I am Parth Shimpi, a first year PhD student at University of Glasgow working with Michael Wemyss and his research group .

Before this, I was at Gonville and Caius College , University of Cambridge where I studied the Mathematics Tripos , completing part III in 2022. I have served as the President of the Archimedeans (Cambridge University Mathematics Society) from 2020 to 2022. You can read more about me here.

My mathematical interests lie in (homological) algebra and algebraic geometry.

Mathematical Writing

  • Derived equivalences in algebra and geometry (2022) [PDF] This was my Part III essay, set by Ian Grojnowski. It introduces derived categories of sheaves and modules and discusses two results concerning equivalences of derived categories-- Beilinson's theorem and Koszul duality.
  • Finitely generated cones and associated moduli spaces (2021) [PDF] This write-up summarises work done in a summer research project with Dan Townsend, supervised by Dhruv Ranganathan. It describes a construction of the moduli space of finitely many points on a line, in a way that brings to light its connection with the ring of quasi-symmetric functions.
I sometimes document my explorations on this website, in the form of articles and bite-sized gists . Hope something catches your interest!
