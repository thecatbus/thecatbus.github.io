Vector Bundles

July 6, 2021 (geometry)

Among the first ideas one encounters in the study of homotopy groups is to consider covering spacesâ€“ things that locally look like products with some discrete topological space \(\Delta\) but can have vastly different global properties than a simple product space. In fact the number of non-trivial covering spaces is a measure of the topological complexity of the space, in a sense made precise by the Galois correspondence of fundamental groups. It is then natural to ask what happens if one replaces \(\Delta\) with more a complicated topological space \(F\): what results is a richer analogue of a covering space called a fiber bundle, or a vector bundle if \(F\) is a vector space.
